The technology next

Human cognition depends primarily on the ability to perceive, interpret, and integrate audio-visual and sensory information. Adding extraordinary perceptual abilities to computers would enable computers to work together with human beings as intimate partners. Researchers are attempting to add more capabilities to computers that will allow them to interact like humans, recognize human presence, talk, listen, or even guess their feelings.

The Blue Eyes technology, developed by IBM at Almaden Research Center, USA, aims at creating computational machines that have perceptual and sensory abilities like those of human beings. It uses non-obtrusive sensing methods, employing most modern video cameras and microphones to identify the user’s actions through the use of imparted sensory abilities. The machine can understand what a user wants, where he is looking at, and even realize his physical or emotional states.

Future applications of the Blue Eyes technology seem to be endless—ranging from a voice-operated lighting system at your household to the most advanced aeroplanes that can communicate with the pilot. It will make the user more productive by performing the expected actions or by providing the expected information. The user can simply sit in his chair and request his Blue Eyes-enabled television set to tune into his favorite programme. Devices like ovens and mixers will do their jobs at users’ will or request.

The Oxygen project

The Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), USA, is currently involved in a project called Oxygen, which it is planning to launch within five or six years. Being a personal network comprising both hardware and software, Oxygen sits atop the global network. Each unit has a small screen, camera, GPS module, Handy 21 mobile phone, infrared detector, two-way radio, and a network-connected device that needs to be programmed as per requirements.

The voice recognition software associated with the device enables you to tell the device your requirement. To add to your curiosity, the device can change into a highly sophisticated television set, handheld PC, beeper, or even a pointing device. It can be mounted anywhere—on the wall, inside your car,..., and so on.  

Emotion mouse

The need of the hour is to design an adaptive smart computer system that could possibly recognize your face, gestures, speed, and even track your eye! An alternative approach to gather personal information is through touch. A smart computer can gain information about the user through touch via a special mouse and determine his emotional state, which, in turn, is related to the task the user is currently involved in with the computer.

Emotional aspects of a person are critical for rational decision-making. In other words, emotion detection is the best approach to an adaptive computer system. By imparting the computer a longitudinal understanding of the emotional state of the user, it could adapt a working style that fits well users’ requirements. This collaborative approach will enhance users’ productivity.

Today, most computer users spend three-fourth of their time scrolling through the screen using an input device called mouse. This has made researchers realise the possibility of detecting users’ emotions through the mouse. So the most obvious place for putting emotion sensors is undoubtedly the mouse.

The computer will determine the emotional state of the user when he simply touches the mouse. Sensors in the mouse sense the physiological attributes, which, in turn, are related to the person’s emotional state. The changes in the temperature of the fingers also form a reliable measure for prediction of the user’s emotions. Other physiological measures that may affect the emotions of the user are heart rate, galvanic skin response (GSR), blood pressure, and galvanic somatic activity (GSA).

Heart rate can be measured through a chest strap sensor. GSA samples can be taken through the mouse driver and GSR samples through fingertips. The input values may then be analyzed and correlated to a predetermined emotional state.

Biometrics promises some revolutionary possibilities that include iris scanning, voice and facial recognition, and other related aspects.

The Suitor

Computers would have been much more powerful, had they gained the perceptual and sensory abilities of the living beings on the earth. What needs to be developed is an intimate relationship between the computer and humans. And the Simple User Interest Tracker (Suitor) is a revolutionary approach in this direction.

By observing the Webpage a netizen is browsing, the Suitor can help fetch more related information at his desktop. By simply noticing where the user’s eyes focus on the computer screen, the Suitor can be more precise in determining his topic of interest. It can even deliver relevant information to a handheld device. The success lies in how much the Suitor can be intimate to the user.

Scroll point mouse

The scroll point mouse was originally designed to satisfy the underlying principles of a good user interface. The user interacts with his computer as usual—by typing, mouse clicking, or reading—and the computer understands his interests based on what he is doing. The scroll point mouse allows the user to scroll the screen up, down, or sideways, without having to steal his visual attention away from what he is currently doing. The user needs not fiddle with scroll bars or move the window up or down, nor will he have to divert his attention frequently for doing such acts. Another technology termed Magic (Manual and Gaze Input Cascaded) Pointing uses eyes in conjunction with hand. When the user stares at a specific area on the screen and touches the mouse simultaneously, the cursor moves automatically to the area that the user is currently looking at, with an accuracy of 1.27 cm (0.5 inch).
